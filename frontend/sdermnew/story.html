How We Started SDERM AI

Three high school students who wanted to make an impact.

The Problem That Inspired This

In 2023, both Srithansh and Michael were dealing with persistent skin issues. They had to wait 2-3 weeks for an appointment, pay expensive consultation fees, and sometimes get conflicting diagnoses from different doctors.

They realized this problem did not only apply to them but millions of people worldwide, particularly those who may come from more underserved communities. Dermatologists and doctors are extremely helpful but as an additional tool, SDERM AI can help.

When the Idea Clicked

Srithansh and Michael, both passionate AI enthusiasts, had been developing machine learning and computer vision projects since middle school, building a strong foundation in advanced technologies. Barghavan, also a skilled programmer, contributed complementary computer science expertise. This made the team capable of tackling complex challenges.

Months of Trial and Error

Building an accurate skin condition classifier was much harder than we initially thought. We spent months cleaning datasets, dealing with biased training data, and trying different model architectures.

We reached out to professors and doctors, asking for feedback and validation. A few became valuable advisors, such as Professor Kai Shu from Emory University and Dr. Anurag Mairal from Stanford University.

Testing Our Vision

In August 2024, we officially founded SDERM AI.

Making it to the top 350 out of thousands of applicants in the World's Most Prestigious Entrepreneurship Competition for High School Students was a huge validation. "We believe that getting top 350 internationally in this competition meant that AI can really help out!" says Srithansh.

When It Finally Worked

By late 2024, our model started consistently hitting over 92% accuracy on standard dermatology datasets. More importantly, we'd solved the bias problem by training on diverse, representative data. It was accurate to up to 15+ different skin types.

The AI could now accurately detect several conditions across different skin tones and demographics.

What We're Working on Now

We're building a tool that provides preliminary skin condition assessments from a phone—especially valuable for those without easy access to dermatological care. We're also working to integrate this with dermatologists’ workflows, offering it as a support tool to enhance their accuracy. While professional diagnosis is essential, our goal is to help people take the first step toward getting the care they need.
